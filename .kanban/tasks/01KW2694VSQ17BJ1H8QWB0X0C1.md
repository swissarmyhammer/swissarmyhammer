## What
Add the top rung: rubric model judgment against the withheld criteria, only on the residual the cheap tiers couldn't decide. Per `ideas/expect.md` §"The Verdict Ladder" Tier 3, §"Tier 3 is the residual-of-the-residual", and §"Reliability and Non-Determinism" (grading hardening).

- In `crates/swissarmyhammer-expect/src/evaluate.rs` (+ grader module):
  - A judgment criterion compiles to `{rubric, anchor: <approved text>, sim_threshold}`. At evaluate: locate evidence → first take **embedding similarity to the anchor**; ≥ threshold ⇒ pass with NO model call. Only on divergence does the judge wake: "does this NEW evidence still satisfy the rubric?" Yes ⇒ passes rubric but evidence changed ⇒ DRIFT (re-approval). No ⇒ fail.
  - The grader is a named sah `model:` (resolved like review/rules; pinned in the golden), DISTINCT from the driving agent. Grade is binary form-filling per criterion (one observable criterion at a time), chain-of-thought + evidence, NOT free-form scoring. Use the ACP grading path (model call; if it must act, an independent ACP subagent) — but the grader must NOT be the same model that drove the run.
  - Optional small **panel** of named models for borderline criteria (config `[model].panel`); disagreement is itself a signal of a vague criterion.
  - **Escalation queue**: criteria resolved with low confidence (below `[approval].escalate_below_confidence`, per-surface tunable) are routed to a human queue, not auto-passed.

## Acceptance Criteria
- [ ] A judgment criterion whose evidence matches the anchor (sim ≥ threshold) passes WITHOUT a model call (assert no model invocation).
- [ ] Divergent-but-rubric-satisfying evidence ⇒ drift; rubric-failing ⇒ fail.
- [ ] Tier 3 runs only on the residual after Tiers 1-2; the grading model is pinned and is not the driver.
- [ ] Low-confidence criteria land in the escalation queue rather than auto-passing; an optional panel records disagreement.

## Tests

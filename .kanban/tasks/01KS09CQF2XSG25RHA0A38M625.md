## What

The expanded AI panel stacks two header rows, wasting vertical space:

1. **`AiPanelShell`'s collapse row** (`apps/kanban-app/ui/src/components/ai-panel-container.tsx`, the `<div className="flex items-center justify-end border-b px-2 py-1">` block in the expanded branch) — holds nothing but the "Collapse AI panel" button.
2. **`AiPanelHeader`** (`apps/kanban-app/ui/src/components/ai-panel.tsx`) — the real header: the "AI" title on the left, the model selector dropdown on the right (`<header className="flex items-center justify-between ... border-b ...">`).

Collapse the two into one. The collapse button belongs on the right of the AI/model-selection header, not in its own row.

### Approach

- **Remove** the dedicated collapse-button row from `AiPanelShell`'s expanded branch in `ai-panel-container.tsx`. Keep the absolute-positioned resize handle and the `<div className="min-h-0 flex-1">{children}</div>` body. The collapsed-rail branch (`!open`) keeps its own "Expand AI panel" button — do not touch it.
- **Thread the collapse callback into the header.** `AiPanelContainer` already has `handleToggle`; it currently passes it to `AiPanelShell` as `onToggle`. Also pass it into `<AiPanel>` as a new `onCollapse` prop.
  - Add `onCollapse: () => void` to `AiPanelProps` and forward it to `AiPanelHeader`.
  - Add `onCollapse: () => void` to `AiPanelHeaderProps`.
- **Render the collapse button inside `AiPanelHeader`**, to the right of the model selector dropdown (after the `</DropdownMenu>`, still inside the `<header>`). Reuse the exact control from the old shell row: `aria-label="Collapse AI panel"`, `variant="ghost"`, `size="icon"`, `<PanelRightCloseIcon className="size-4" />`. Wrap the selector + collapse button in a flex group so both sit on the right while "AI" stays left. Move the `PanelRightCloseIcon` import from `ai-panel-container.tsx` to `ai-panel.tsx` if it is no longer used by the container.
- The `border-b` separator is preserved — `AiPanelHeader`'s `<header>` already carries `border-b`.

Keep the `Container`/`View` split intact: `AiPanelContainer` still owns the open-state; `AiPanelHeader` only renders a button wired to the passed `onCollapse` callback, exactly as it already does with `onSelectModel`.

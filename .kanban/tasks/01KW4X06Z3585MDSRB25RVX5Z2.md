title: Spec-timeout teardown should actively cancel the in-flight ACP session (Finding 3)

## What
Confirmed during ^z8rwhhj (wire drive_and_revalidate into the live tool): the spec-timeout stop in `drive_and_revalidate` (`crates/swissarmyhammer-expect/src/drive.rs`) bounds the drive with `tokio::time::timeout`, which on elapse DROPS the drive future. Dropping tears down the `AgentPool` workers but does NOT send `session/cancel` for the in-flight ACP session — `AgentPool`'s `Drop` is synchronous and cannot await an async cancel over the connection it is dropping. The pool's idle/ceiling cancel path (its liveness supervisor) is the only path that actively cancels today, and that path is not on the spec-timeout teardown.

A reproducing test exists and was adjusted to pin the guaranteed behavior (prompt typed `ExpectError::Timeout`, no hang): `drive::tests::spec_timeout_over_an_acp_session_aborts_the_in_flight_drive`. It deliberately does NOT assert the cancel (it doesn't happen). An earlier version asserting the cancel FAILED, confirming the gap.

## Acceptance Criteria
- [ ] On spec-timeout, the in-flight ACP session is actively cancelled (`session/cancel` sent to the agent), not only its pool workers aborted.
- [ ] A real-pipeline test over a mock-ACP `StallingAgent` (mirror `spec_timeout_over_an_acp_session_aborts_the_in_flight_drive`) asserts the cancel is sent, in addition to the typed timeout.
- [ ] No hang and no regression to the existing stop-condition tests.

## Notes
Likely needs the spec-timeout to *actively* cancel before/while dropping (e.g. drive_and_revalidate or AcpGoalDriver issuing the cancel on the timeout branch), rather than relying on `AgentPool` `Drop`. Touches the engine (`drive.rs`) and possibly `swissarmyhammer-validators` `AgentPool` teardown.
